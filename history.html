My History


under 12

I was born in Kyoto ciry, Japan in 1998.

Since my mother is a music teacher, I went to Musicals and Operas so many times.

That's why I love to sing and listen to music even now.

When I was elementary student, there was a test of science. In that test, everyone except me could not get 100% score and I was praised by my teacher.

That was very happy event, so after that, I started studying hard in order to get as high score as I can.

There's another thing to notice. There was a Ekiden competition of all elementary schools in Kyoto-city.

I was one of the team which represent my school. The result was not good. We couldn't go to the final.

That also made me decide to belong to track&field club in junior high school.

junior high school and high school

Thanks to the effort and luck I had, I could enter famous junior high school in Kyoto.

The school system was six-year high school education system, so I spent 6 years in that school.

As I said before, I decided to belong to track&field club and I mainly ran 1500m or 800m.

In the first year, I could not do English test well and i had to do the supplementary lesson with my teacher.

However after that, I got used to use English and I became to be able to get high score in English exam.

In the third year, I went to Australia ( that was first time I went abroad) and I was so fascinated by the magnificent and beautiful nature, interesting culture, and indegenous food.

After the experience, I really wanted to go anywhere I can and experience such a new world.

Therefore, I went to Fiji and Canada for a month to study English and feel other cultures.

That deepened my knowledge of English and other culture.

University era

While learning English, One thing came up with my mind, "I want to learn other language!"

Then, I love to watch Opera and Musical, so I decided to learn German language as my major, in order to be able to understand what they sing or say in Opera and Musical.

Learning German was so hard to me, because at that time I am interested in a cappella club activity and I worked a lot as a part-time job.

However, fortunately, I passed the test and I was choosen as a exchange student to Goethe University, which is located in Frankfurt, Germany.

From September 2019, I stayed in the domitory and I started new life in Germany.

There were so many difficulties such as culture-shock and the language-barrier, but I gradually got accustomed to the life in Germany.

When the time to go back to Japan came in March 2020, I really missed the life in Germany.

Then the Covid-era came.

I did not know what to do and in which industry I should work, so I decided to do anything I want.

I played the guiter, I started learning Chinese, and the last thing I started was programming.
